Structural improvements in the pattern of food production by the rural poor in the third world
will have to be achieved in the years ahead since rural development and food self-reliance
have at long last been recognized as the number one development priority by the political leaders
in the South and in the North.
Mr. President, under your leadership, Italy has achieved a leading position in international development cooperation.
In a speech you made two years ago, you said, and I quote from your speech,
the recurrent nature of droughts and the simple analysis of nutritional levels should be sufficient
to prompt us to re-examine the workings of the whole world food production system.
And you called for a charter of human rights to food security,
a world pack which would link up the productive investment capacities
and the necessary policy adjustments of both the North and the South
in a massive common endeavor aimed at mobilizing, and I quote you again Mr. Prime Minister,
the collective will of nations and states in order to durably remove from the face of the earth
the specter of famine.
This is a great and noble vision indeed, Mr. President.
I wonder if next year, which will be the 10th anniversary of IFAD,
that this year could not also be a very appropriate opportunity for a political initiative by Italy
and other countries in the directions you indicated so clearly and so forcefully two years ago.
Times are changing and the awareness of world food problems is increasing dramatically in most countries,
both in the North and in the South.
A consensus has emerged in particular at the UN special session on Africa,
and now there appears to be a unique opportunity for real political action to finally tackle the world food problem.
Mr. President, let me conclude on a note of special appreciation.
The Italian government was one of the first contributors to the second replenishment of IFAD's resources.
It has pledged major support to the fund's special program for sub-Saharan African countries affected by drought and desertification.
It has provided resources for other special initiatives undertaken within the fund.
It is also in the process, after some delay, of arranging for permanent headquarter facilities
to be provided to IFAD in compliance with the headquarters agreement.
We are pleased at the assurance we have been given that these facilities will become available
in conjunction with the celebration of IFAD's 10th anniversary celebration.
