\section{Democracy Index}

There are plenty of sources for measuring democracy levels. Our world in data mainly uses six: V-Dem, the Lexical Index of Electoral Democracy (LIED) by Skaaning et al. (2015), Freedom House's (FH) Freedom in the World index, Bertelsmann Transformation Index (BTI) by the Bertelsmann Foundation, Economist Intelligence Unit's (EIU) Democracy Index, and Polity by the Center for Systemic Peace \cite{herre_democracy_2025}. Out of this six datasets, only V-Dem and LIED have data between 1946 and 2025, but V-Dem offer more granularity and is generally considered a more complete and robust dataset, and its the best choice if we are interested in big and small differences in varieties of democracy, far into the past, and want to use country experts to measure characteristics of political systems that are difficult to observe.

For these reasons, the dataset I have chosen is V-Dem.

All data is available for download at \url{https://v-dem.net/data/the-v-dem-dataset/} or directly as an R package.
