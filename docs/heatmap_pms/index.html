Vocabulary Overlap and Jaccard Similarity

For each politician, the 1000 most frequent words used across their speeches are extracted as a set. The Overlap matrix reports how many of these top-1000 words two politicians have in common. The Jaccard Similarity matrix normalizes this by the size of the union of the two sets, giving a score between 0 and 1 that is comparable across pairs regardless of vocabulary size.

Ordering

Politicians are ordered along both axes using hierarchical clustering (average linkage) on the Jaccard distance (1 − Jaccard similarity). This places politicians with more similar vocabularies next to each other, so clusters of stylistically or thematically related speakers appear as visible blocks along the diagonal.
