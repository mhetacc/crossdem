Measure of Textual Lexical Diversity (MTLD)

Lexical diversity is one aspect of 'lexical richness' and refers to the ratio of different unique word stems (types) to the total number of words (tokens).
The Measure of Textual Lexical Diversity (MTLD) is computed as the mean length of sequential words in a text that maintains a minimum threshold Type-Token Ratio (TTR) score. Compared to TTR is agnostic to text length.

The metric shows how rich, how varied, a text's vocabulary is.

Political Groups

All PMs' MTLD values are aggregated in three political areas: left (which aggregates left and center left), right (which aggregates right and center right), and center. Moreover, "Combined" aggregates all of them into one.

Line Modes

Three modes for the MTLD line chart are available: Exact shows the exact median (or average) for each year.
Smoothed applies a certain degree of Gaussian smoothing to help graph readability. The smoothing degree can be chosen via a slider.
Trend shows a spline regression, thus trying to "fill-in" the gaps in the data by fitting a non-linear regression on it. With a slider it is possibile to choose the amount of steps (of knots) for the spline regression.

V-Dem Indices

The Democracy Indices by V-Dem are democracy indices published by the V-Dem Institute that describe qualities of different democracies. The dataset focuses on both qualitative and quantitative measurements of democracy, and its curated by a team of experts.
V-Dem version: 16.

In the graph, six indices are extracted:

  • Electoral democracy: measures to what extent is the ideal of electoral democracy in its fullest sense achieved. Aggregates indices such as freedom of association and expression, and cleanness of elections (id: v2x_polyarchy);
  • Liberal democracy: the liberal principle of democracy emphasizes the importance of protecting individual and minority rights against the tyranny of the state and the tyranny of the majority (id: v2x_libdem);
  • Participatory democracy: the participatory principle of democracy emphasizes active participation by citizens in all political processes, electoral and non-electoral. Thus, direct rule by citizens scores higher (id: v2x_partipdem);
  • Elections quality: measures how free and fair the elections are, connoted by an absence of registration fraud, systematic irregularities, government intimidation of the opposition, vote buying, and election violence (id: v2xel_frefair);
  • Fairness of law and individual liberties: measures to what extent are laws transparent and rigorously enforced and public administration impartial, and to what extent do citizens enjoy access to justice, secure property rights, freedom from forced labor, freedom of movement, physical integrity rights, and freedom of religion (id: v2xcl_rol);
  • Scrutiny of executives: measures to what extent are the legislature and government agencies ,such as comptroller general, general prosecutor, or ombudsman, capable of questioning, investigating, and exercising oversight over the executive (id: v2xlg_legcon).
